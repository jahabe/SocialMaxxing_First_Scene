## Inspiration
COVID and generative AI didn't just disrupt school schedules. They interrupted something far more essential: the social education that only comes from being around other people. The years children spend learning how to introduce themselves, disagree without fighting, share, and read a room were never taught through textbooks. They were learned through interaction between adults and children, between friends and family. Those were years that quietly slipped away. Social Maxxing was built out of grief for that lost time, and a genuine desire to give kids back what they missed.

## What it does
SocialMaxxing is a social etiquette game built in Unity that teaches children how to navigate real-world interactions through an otherworldly storyline and choice-based gameplay. 

The player is an alien who has just landed on Earth. To avoid being exposed, they must learn to blend in with the humans around them. Across three settings (school, cafe, and home), they encounter characters and must choose how to respond: how to introduce themselves, how to handle disagreement, how to share. Every choice gets instant feedback (green for respectful responses, red for harmful ones) with the progress bar. The twist? You'll just have to play to find out!

## How we built it
We built SocialMaxxing entirely in Unity, combining custom C# scripts with Unity's animation and UI systems to create an interactive, choice-based experience. The game uses ShaderLab and HLSL for visual styling, giving it the hand-drawn, storybook aesthetic you see throughout.

Unity assets we utilized: [School (setting)](https://assetstore.unity.com/packages/3d/environments/school-assets-146253#publisher), [Start scene map](https://assetstore.unity.com/packages/3d/environments/prototype-map-315588)

UI Images: hand-drawn by the team and refined with ChatGPT
